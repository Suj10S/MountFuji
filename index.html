Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about studying abroad

Requirements vary by country and institution, but generally include: completed secondary education (for undergraduate) or bachelor's degree (for graduate), proof of language proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL for English-speaking countries, JLPT for Japan, etc.), financial proof showing ability to cover tuition and living expenses, and a clean academic record. Some programs may also require standardized tests like SAT, GRE, or GMAT.

IELTS is crucial for studying in English-speaking countries like the UK, Australia, Canada, and New Zealand. Most universities require a minimum IELTS score (typically 6.0-7.0 for undergraduate and 6.5-7.5 for postgraduate programs). A good IELTS score not only fulfills admission requirements but also strengthens your visa application. We provide comprehensive IELTS preparation to help you achieve your target score.

It depends on the country and program. For English-speaking countries, IELTS/TOEFL is sufficient. For non-English speaking countries like Japan, Germany, or France, you may need proficiency in the local language, especially for programs taught in that language. However, many universities now offer English-taught programs. Basic knowledge of the local language is always beneficial for daily life and cultural integration.

Many scholarship options are available: government scholarships (like Chevening in UK, Fulbright in US), university-specific scholarships, private foundation scholarships, and country-specific scholarships (like MEXT for Japan). Scholarships may cover full or partial tuition, living expenses, or both. We help identify and apply for scholarships that match your profile and significantly reduce your financial burden.

Visa processing times vary by country. Generally, it takes 3-6 months total. For countries like the USA, student visa processing can take 2-3 months. For the UK, it's typically 3-4 weeks. For Japan, the process involves first obtaining a Certificate of Eligibility (1-3 months) followed by the visa application (1-2 weeks). We recommend starting the process 6 months before your intended departure date to ensure smooth processing.

Most countries allow international students to work part-time with certain restrictions. In Australia, students can work up to 40 hours per fortnight during semester and unlimited during breaks. In the UK, students can work up to 20 hours per week during term time. In Canada, students can work up to 20 hours per week during academic sessions. Part-time jobs typically help cover living expenses while gaining valuable work experience.

Our support continues after your arrival! We provide airport pickup assistance, help with initial accommodation setup, guidance for opening bank accounts and getting phone connections, assistance with residence registration and health insurance, ongoing academic and personal counseling, job search support for part-time work, and 24/7 emergency contact support. We're committed to your success throughout your entire journey abroad.
